# 漢字 Kanji

  • Kanji was imported from China more than 1500 years ago when there was no writing system in Japan.
  • Originally, hiragana and katakana were just simplified kanji to represent the Japanese sound system.
  • Kanji has multiple readings, on-yomi (Chinese readings) and kun-yomi (Japanese readings).
  • There are 2136 joyo kanji, or commonly used kanji. Native Japanese are expected to know them.
  • For daily lives in Japan, you need to remember the most common 1000 kanji at least.
